## Overview

Safar Logistics & Warehousing dispatches 90,000–150,000 trucks daily. Before every dispatch, a critical safety checkpoint verifies that each truck is properly loaded and secured. This process was digitized in 2018 via an internal tool called **LoadX** — but despite being a digital tool, the entire workflow still ran on manual effort, spreadsheets, and delayed communication.

This case study defines the full business analysis for upgrading LoadX into a structured, automated, and data-driven platform.

---

## Problem Statement

- Associates could process only **300 images/day** against a daily volume of **100,000+** — creating a significant throughput bottleneck
- Job allocation was done manually by managers, consuming time that should go toward performance management
- Audit feedback took **1–2 days** to reach associates, by which point errors had already repeated
- The audit team reviewed only **5–10% of jobs** due to volume constraints, leaving quality largely unchecked
- KPIs were calculated manually in Excel with no real-time visibility for any role
- No centralized data store — job, audit, and performance records were scattered across spreadsheets

---

## Projected Impact

| Metric | As-Is | To-Be |
|---|---|---|
| Associate Throughput | 300 jobs/day | 840 jobs/day **(3× increase)** |
| Accuracy | ~90% | **97%** |
| Average TAT | ~84 seconds | **~30 seconds** |
| Feedback Loop | 1–2 days | **Under 5 minutes** |
| Audit Coverage | 5–10% | **10–15%** |
| Reporting | Manual (Excel) | **Real-time dashboards** |
